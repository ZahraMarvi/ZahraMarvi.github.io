Research Summary

I am currently a Postdoctoral Associate at the University of Minnesota Twin Cities, working with Prof. Andrew Alleyne and Prof. Francesco Bullo. My research focuses on developing control frameworks that guarantee safety while remaining computationally scalable, with applications in energy systems. I received my Ph.D. from Michigan State University, where I developed safe and verifiable reinforcement learning algorithms for the optimal control of dynamical systems under model and environmental uncertainty, with applications to autonomous systems. Prior to my Ph.D., I received my M.Sc. and B.Sc. degrees from K. N. Toosi University of Technology, where I gained hands-on experience in mechatronic design, implementation, and control of robotic systems.
